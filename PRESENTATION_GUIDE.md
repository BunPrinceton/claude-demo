# How to Present This Demo to Your Dad

## The Setup (30 seconds)

**What to say:**
> "Dad, I want to show you something cool. This is Claude - an AI assistant I've been using. But instead of me TELLING you what it can do, I'm going to SHOW you.
>
> Here's the kicker: This demo program you're about to see? Claude wrote it. In about 30 seconds. The entire thing. Let's run it."

**Then launch:**
```bash
cd /Users/bds2/Documents/claude-demo
python3 demo.py
```

## During the Demo

### Let it speak for itself
- Don't talk over it - the demo is self-explanatory
- Let your dad read and interact at his own pace
- Watch for the moments that land (usually the meta intro and the problem-solving)

### Watch for reactions
- **Skeptical face**: Pause and say "Want to see the actual code that's running?"
- **Impressed face**: Say nothing, let it keep going
- **Confused face**: Offer to explain what just happened
- **Engaged face**: Perfect, demo is working

## After the Demo (The Real Pitch)

### Immediate follow-up
**Don't say:** "So, wasn't that cool?"
**Do say:** "Want to ask it something yourself?"

### Live Claude Session
Open Claude (the actual interface) and let him:
1. **Ask it a question** about something he's actually interested in
2. **Give it a problem** he's currently working on
3. **Show it an image** of something and watch it analyze

### Examples based on interests:

**If he's into business/finance:**
- "Ask Claude to analyze a market trend"
- "Have it explain a complex financial concept"
- "Upload a business article and ask for key insights"

**If he's technical:**
- "Ask it to explain how something works (blockchain, AI, quantum computing)"
- "Have it write a script to solve a real problem"
- "Show it some code and ask it to explain or improve it"

**If he's into home/practical stuff:**
- "Take a photo of something broken and ask how to fix it"
- "Ask for a step-by-step guide for a project"
- "Have it help plan something (renovation, trip, budget)"

**If he's skeptical:**
- "Ask it a hard question - something you think will stump it"
- "Give it a logic puzzle"
- "Ask it to explain something complex in simple terms"

## The Close

### The soft close
"Pretty cool, right? It's been helpful for me with [your actual use case]. Let me know if you want to try it out - I can help you get started."

## Secret Weapon: The Personal Touch

The best demo is one that solves HIS problem:
- Find out what he's working on
- What he's curious about
- What he finds annoying or time-consuming
- Then show Claude handling that SPECIFIC thing

**Generic demo**: "Here's what AI can do"
**Personal demo**: "Here's AI solving YOUR problem"

The second one wins every time.
